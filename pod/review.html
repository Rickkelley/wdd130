Podcast name "The Magnus Archives"

Synopsis: The Magnus Archives is a weekly horror fiction anthology podcast examining what lurks in the archives of the Magnus Institute, an organization dedicated to researching the esoteric and the weird. Join new head archivist Jonathan Sims as he attempts to bring a seemingly neglected collection of supernatural statements up to date, converting them to audio and supplementing them with follow-up work from his small but dedicated team

The review: If you enjoy reading a good horror story, think a good Stephen King novel for an example, then you will most likely also enjoy this. As I was interviewing people, just prior to my publishing of this site, a vast majority of them listed this as on of their top 3 podcasts. We the staff her at let's talk podcasts have also listened to this one, and it come highly recommended by them as well.

If you check their webpage, The Magnus Archives Reviews. you will find we are not alone there are numerous rave reviews from various other sources as well. This podcast alone is downloaded over 2.5 million times each month.

Podcast name "Crime Junkie"

Synopsis: Crime Junkie is a weekly true crime podcast dedicated to giving you a fix. Each of the weekly podcasts hosted by Ashley Flowers. Each episode is focused on a different crime.

The review: If you are interested in crime stories, especially true crime then this is a good choice for you. It is estimated that approximately 50% of the top 10 most popular podcasts in the world are about true. crime. The host of this podcast, Ashely Flowers, we felt does a really good job presenting things.

This podcast has a new episode released every Monday, and is listed in the top 10 most popular podcasts right now. While most of the staff here didn't find it appealing, a few million other people do.
